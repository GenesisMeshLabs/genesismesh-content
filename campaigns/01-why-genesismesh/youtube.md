**Description:**
Systems connect fine. They don't carry trust across the line that separates them. When a request crosses an organizational boundary, every system on the other side has to re-verify, re-interpret, or just assume — and "assume" is where compromised suppliers, stale permissions, and unauditable decisions live.

Genesis Mesh is a protocol that makes trust decisions explicit, signed, and portable: a Network Authority issues self-contained evidence when it recognizes or authorizes another, verifiable offline, revocable with the same proof it was granted with. Not a marketplace. Not a reputation score. Not a shared registry — a protocol layer for cross-boundary trust.
