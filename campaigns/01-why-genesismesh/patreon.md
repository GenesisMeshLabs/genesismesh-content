# Genesis Mesh Portable Trust Layer

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fv1XebByCbE

We published the first video in the Genesis Mesh series this week — an introduction to the core problem the project exists to solve.

**The objective behind this video**

Most people who work with distributed systems understand, at some level, that trust is broken at organizational boundaries. But "broken" is a vague word. What we wanted this video to show, concretely, is what the break looks like in practice: a compromised supplier that keeps making authorized requests because nothing carries the revocation downstream. A long-running automation with permissions no one reviewed after the original context expired. An audit that can confirm a decision was made but cannot prove why it was authorized.

The instinct when you see that pattern is to add a central authority — a shared identity provider, a registry, a broker everyone consults. We've spent a lot of time thinking about why that instinct is wrong, and why it replaces one problem (unverifiable trust) with a different, harder one (centralized control over decisions that belong to independent organizations).

Genesis Mesh starts from a different premise: trust should be carried by the protocol, not assumed by the connectivity layer. This video is the beginning of that argument. Every video in this series builds from here — on what it means for trust to be portable, sovereign, auditable, and revocable. This first one sets the vocabulary for the rest.

We're building this in the open, and the recognition network that forms on top of the protocol is what will make it durable. Thank you for being part of it from the beginning.
