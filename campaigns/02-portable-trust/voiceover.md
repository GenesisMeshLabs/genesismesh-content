Zero trust tells us to verify every request. But most zero trust systems still live inside one organization boundary.

Portable trust answers the harder question: what happens when trust has to cross that boundary?

A portable trust decision is explicit. It was made at a specific time, by a specific authority, for a specific purpose. It is self-contained, so another party can verify it offline. And it is revocable, so the issuing authority can withdraw it with proof.

Genesis Mesh turns that idea into protocol artifacts: signed attestations, agreements, evidence records, and revocations that can move between independent systems.

Trust should move with the decision, not remain trapped inside the system that made it.
