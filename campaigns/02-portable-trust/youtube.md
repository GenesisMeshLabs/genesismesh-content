**Description:**
Zero trust solves verification inside your perimeter. It doesn't solve what happens when a request originates outside it. Shared secrets, mutual TLS, and federated identity all work within a narrow scope — none of them carry a trust decision from one organization to another without dragging the issuing authority's infrastructure into every verification.

Portable trust is explicit, self-contained, and revocable: a decision made by a specific authority, at a specific time, recorded in a signed artifact that any party with the right public key can verify offline. Genesis Mesh implements this as a protocol — Ed25519-signed, canonical JSON, with SDKs in TypeScript, Go, and .NET producing identical artifacts.
