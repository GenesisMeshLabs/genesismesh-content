**Description:**
Standard enterprise integration usually means one company adopts the other's identity system, or both adopt a third-party broker neither fully controls. Either way, one party's access control and revocation decisions become entangled with the other's infrastructure — easy to agree to commercially, hard to unwind technically.

Genesis Mesh enables integration through explicit, bilateral recognition instead: each enterprise's Network Authority issues a signed recognition treaty defining exactly what it recognizes in the other, under what terms, for how long. Cross-boundary requests carry signed evidence checked against that treaty — if one side's credentials are revoked, the other detects it on next verification, with its own systems unaffected. It's the technical analog of a contract: explicit terms, a defined scope, and a revocation mechanism either party can exercise unilaterally.
