**Description:**
Data governance policies look complete on paper. The enforcement layer beneath them is usually thinner than it looks: access logs show what was accessed, policy engines show what the rules said at the time — but neither proves who granted access, under what delegation, or whether the stated purpose matched the actual access. After the fact, an audit answer is usually assembled from partial records, which is not the same as being proven.

Genesis Mesh treats a data access event as a trust decision: a signed DataUsage disclosure record, timestamped, including the stated purpose and policy terms, produced at the moment of access rather than reconstructed later. It's verifiable offline by anyone holding the issuing authority's public key — built for cross-organizational data sharing under agreements like GDPR data processing terms, where a regulator needs proof, not a reconstruction. It does not catalog or translate the data itself — only the authorization context that travels with it.
