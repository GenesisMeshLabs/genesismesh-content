Trust is not a binary state that you set once and forget. It is a lifecycle.

Systems recognize each other, cooperate under that recognition, and then circumstances change. A partner is compromised. A contract expires. A key is rotated by the wrong party.

Most trust systems are designed to say yes. They are much weaker when they need to say no.

Genesis Mesh treats recognition and revocation symmetrically. Both are explicit, signed operations. A recognition treaty declares the terms. A revocation artifact declares when trust ended, why, and under whose key.

The ability to cut trust off with proof is not an optional security feature. It is a prerequisite for operating across boundaries.
