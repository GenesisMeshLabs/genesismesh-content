**Description:**
Most trust systems are built to say yes. Almost none of them are built to say no — cleanly, provably, and on demand. When a partner is compromised, a contract expires, or a key is rotated by the wrong party, revocation is usually an afterthought: rotate the key and hope nothing downstream cached it.

Genesis Mesh treats recognition and revocation as symmetric, signed operations using the same format, the same key, and the same distribution path. A revocation artifact doesn't just stop access — it records why trust ended, when, and under whose key, in a form that can be verified years later. That's the difference between a session timing out and trust being provably cut off.
