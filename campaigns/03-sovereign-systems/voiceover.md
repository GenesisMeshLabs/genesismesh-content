Every cross-boundary trust architecture eventually asks the same question: who is in charge?

Two financial institutions may need to cooperate, but neither wants the other identity provider in its trust chain. A shared broker looks simple, until both organizations accept someone else key management, uptime, and revocation rules.

Sovereign systems reject that tradeoff. Each operator keeps its own keys, its own recognition policy, and its own revocation decisions.

Genesis Mesh lets sovereigns cooperate through signed recognition, not centralized control. They can recognize one another, exchange attestations, and revoke trust without surrendering local authority.

The value is not isolation. The value is joining a recognition network without giving up sovereignty.
