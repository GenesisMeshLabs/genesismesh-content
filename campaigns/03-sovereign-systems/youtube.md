**Description:**
Every cross-boundary trust architecture eventually asks: who is in charge? The standard answer — a shared broker — buys consistency at the cost of authority transfer. Centralizing trust centralizes control: whoever runs the central authority decides what gets recognized and what gets revoked.

Sovereign systems reject that structure. Each operator keeps its own signing keys, its own recognition policy, and the unilateral ability to revoke — while still cooperating through explicit, bilateral recognition treaties with other sovereigns. Genesis Mesh builds this in at the protocol level: every deployment is an independent Network Authority with its own genesis block and key pair. No master registry. No parent authority.
