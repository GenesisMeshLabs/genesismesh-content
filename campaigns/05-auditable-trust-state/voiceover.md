After an incident, the question is never only what happened. It is why that action was allowed to happen.

Logs can show that a request occurred. Policies can suggest what should have been allowed. But neither is the same as signed evidence of the trust state at that moment.

Auditable trust state means every significant trust decision leaves a signed artifact: who trusted whom, what it permitted, when it was issued, and under which key.

Genesis Mesh produces that evidence as protocol output. Agreements, attestations, boundary decisions, consensus outcomes, and data usage disclosures become verifiable records.

The audit trail is not reconstructed after the fact. It is created when the trust decision happens.
